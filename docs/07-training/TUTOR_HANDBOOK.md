## <a name="documenting-sessions"></a>Documenting Sessions

### Why Session Notes Matter

- **Student accountability** - Track what was covered
- **Progress tracking** - Show improvement over time
- **Intervention planning** - Identify struggling students
- **Program metrics** - Demonstrate effectiveness
- **Leadership visibility** - Data-driven decisions

**Target:** 95%+ completion rate (sessions with notes)

### When to Document

**Timing:** Within 24 hours of session completion

**Access:** Dashboard ‚Üí "Session Notes" ‚Üí Select appointment

### Session Notes Form

#### 1. Actual Duration

Enter actual minutes spent (vs scheduled)
- Example: Scheduled 60 min, went 70 min ‚Üí Enter 70
- Helps track accurate tutoring hours

#### 2. Materials Covered (Rich Text)

**What to include:**
- Specific chapters/pages from textbook
- Topics discussed
- Exercises completed
- Materials reviewed

**Example:**
```
- Reviewed Chapter 5: Past tense verbs
- Practiced conjugations for 15 common verbs
- Completed exercises 1-10 on page 45
- Discussed cultural context of formal vs informal speech
```

#### 3. Focus Areas (Checkboxes)

Select all that apply:
- ‚òê Vocabulary
- ‚òê Grammar
- ‚òê Listening
- ‚òê Reading
- ‚òê Speaking
- ‚òê Writing
- ‚òê DLPT Prep

#### 4. Student Performance (Required)

Rate overall session performance:
- ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **Excellent** - Mastered material, ready to advance
- ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **Good** - Solid understanding, minor review needed
- ‚≠ê‚≠ê **Satisfactory** - Grasped basics, needs more practice
- ‚≠ê **Needs Improvement** - Struggled, requires additional support

#### 5. Student Participation

Engagement level:
- üòä **Engaged** - Active, asking questions, practicing
- üòê **Average** - Present, participating when prompted
- üò¥ **Distracted** - Unfocused, disengaged

#### 6. Homework Assigned (Rich Text)

Specific tasks for student to complete:

**Example:**
```
- Complete exercises 11-20 on pages 46-47
- Write 10 sentences using past tense verbs
- Listen to audio tracks 5-7, note new vocabulary
- Review verb conjugation chart before next session
```

#### 7. Next Session Goals

What to focus on next time:

**Example:**
```
- Begin Chapter 6: Future tense
- Review homework exercises
- Practice conversational scenarios
- Introduce new vocabulary set (20 words)
```

#### 8. Challenges Observed

Specific difficulties noticed:

**Example:**
```
- Struggles with verb ending pronunciation
- Confuses masculine/feminine noun forms
- Needs more confidence in speaking
- Writing mechanics need work (accent marks)
```

#### 9. Recommendations

Actions to improve:

**Example:**
```
- Increase speaking practice time
- Assign additional grammar exercises
- Recommend language lab hours
- Consider peer study group
```

#### 10. Attached Resources (Optional)

Links to materials:
- Handouts you provided
- Online resources mentioned
- Practice websites
- Video tutorials

### Completing Session Notes

**Steps:**
1. Fill all required fields (1-6 minimum)
2. Add optional fields as relevant (7-10)
3. Click **[Save Notes]**
4. Confirmation message appears
5. Student can view notes in their progress tracker

**Notes are permanent** - Cannot be deleted, only edited within 24 hours

## <a name="best-practices"></a>Best Practices for Effective Tutoring

### Before the Session

‚úÖ **Review student profile** (5 minutes before)
- Check previous session notes
- Note homework assigned last time
- Review focus areas student requested

‚úÖ **Prepare materials**
- Textbook, handouts, exercises
- Have lesson plan ready
- Extra practice problems prepared

‚úÖ **Check logistics**
- In-person: Room ready, materials available
- Online: Test meeting link, tech working

‚úÖ **Be on time**
- Arrive 5 minutes early
- If running late, email student immediately

### During the Session

‚úÖ **Start with review** (10-15 minutes)
- Check homework from last session
- Review previous material
- Answer questions before new content

‚úÖ **Focus on student goals**
- Address their requested focus areas first
- Adapt to their needs, not rigid plan
- Ask what they want to work on

‚úÖ **Interactive practice**
- Don't just lecture
- Have student speak, write, practice
- Correct mistakes gently
- Encourage questions

‚úÖ **Track time**
- Monitor session duration
- Pace yourself to cover planned topics
- Leave 5 minutes for wrap-up

‚úÖ **Cultural context**
- Explain cultural nuances
- Real-world application
- Why certain phrases are used

### Session Structure (Recommended)

**For 60-minute session:**
- 0-10 min: Review homework, Q&A
- 10-40 min: New material and practice
- 40-55 min: Exercises and conversation
- 55-60 min: Homework assignment and wrap-up

### After the Session

‚úÖ **Document immediately** (while fresh)
- Complete session notes within 24 hours
- Be specific and detailed
- Note student strengths and weaknesses

‚úÖ **Follow up as needed**
- Email additional resources
- Clarify homework if student asks
- Answer questions that came up

‚úÖ **Plan next session**
- Think about progression
- Prepare materials in advance
- Identify what to focus on next

### Communication Tips

**With Students:**
- Be encouraging and supportive
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate improvements
- Create safe space for mistakes

**With Leadership:**
- Escalate concerns early
- Document everything
- Respond to requests promptly
- Attend tutor meetings

**With Other Tutors:**
- Share effective techniques
- Coordinate on shared students
- Help each other cover absences
- Maintain professionalism

## <a name="troubleshooting"></a>Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**"I can't find session notes form for completed appointment"**

**Fix:**
1. Go to Dashboard
2. Click "Session Notes"
3. Dropdown shows completed appointments without notes
4. Select appointment
5. If not there, appointment may not be marked "Completed" yet

**"Student says they didn't receive my cancellation notice"**

**Check:**
- Email actually sent? (Check sent items)
- Student's email correct in system?
- Their spam folder?

**Contact helpdesk if emails not sending**

**"I need to change my availability"**

**Process:**
- Email Tutor Chief with requested changes
- Include: Days/times to add or remove
- Reason for change
- How many weeks

**"Student marked no-show but they were there"**

**Fix:**
1. Contact admin immediately
2. Provide evidence (email, your notes)
3. Admin can correct status
4. Document incident

**"Session notes won't save"**

**Possible causes:**
- Required fields not filled
- Connection issue
- Session timeout

**Fix:**
- Copy text to notepad (don't lose it!)
- Refresh browser
- Fill required fields (marked with *)
- Try again
- Contact helpdesk if persists

## Analytics & Metrics

### Your Dashboard

**Personal Statistics:**
- Sessions completed this week/month
- Total hours taught
- Utilization rate (hours used / max hours)
- Average performance ratings from session notes
- No-show rate for your appointments

### Understanding Utilization

**Target:** 60-80% utilization

- **<50%** - Underutilized (can take more students)
- **50-70%** - Good range (balanced workload)
- **70-90%** - Optimal (high engagement, sustainable)
- **>90%** - Over-utilized (risk of burnout)

**Formula:** (Actual Hours / Max Hours) √ó 100

### Performance Metrics

**Session Notes Completion:**
- Target: 95%+ within 24 hours
- Your rate shown on dashboard
- Leadership monitors this

**Student Performance Distribution:**
- How many "Excellent" vs "Needs Improvement"
- Helps identify if you're being too easy/hard
- Should match bell curve generally

**No-Show Rate:**
- Should be <10% for your appointments
- Higher rate may indicate communication issues
- Document patterns and discuss with chief
